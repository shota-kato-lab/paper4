What is this? ▾

This page is a static reference catalogue of the 22 indicators in the Bettencourt 2007 urban-scaling panel, paired with the Paper 4 ladder formula

β = 1 ± 1 / [H · ln(2H + 1)],   H ∈ ℕ

What is the scaling exponent β and how is it predicted? ▾

β describes how a city-level indicator (e.g., GDP, patent count, road length, gasoline consumption) scales with city population at a single point in time. If City B has 10× the population of City A, the indicator is approximately 10β times larger. This is a cross-sectional comparison between cities, not a year-over-year growth rate.

Foundational theory: Scaling Exponent Hierarchy Law (author's original framework)

β = 1 ± 1 / [H · ln(2H + 1)],   H ∈ ℕ

H ∈ {1, …, 8} = relay-depth steps. H equals the number of 【】 boxes in the coordination chain for the indicator (an integer count of structural layers, not the number of arrows between them). H = 1 = direct individual coordination (a single 【box】); H = 8 = a deeply stacked 8-layer hierarchy. The integer H and the sign (±) are mathematically determined by the structure of coordination (network type, substrate constraint), assigned a priori per Paper 4 V261 §S-PanelOrigins. No coefficient is fitted against observed β.

Notation. βpred = ladder prediction from H + branch sign. βobs = observed slope from per-city (N, Y) data. The catalogue compares the two but never uses βobs to infer H.

For each indicator, the catalogue displays the author's structural assignment (industry classification, branch sign, relay-depth H) per Paper 4 V261 §S-PanelOrigins together with the observed βobs 95% CI from the Bettencourt 2007 PNAS supplement (Table S1). No coefficient is fitted to data. No H is inferred from a graph or from an (N, Y) panel.

Five rows (gasoline sales, gasoline stations, AIDS deaths, inventors, and the Bettencourt 2007 legacy patents panel) are reported under the Tier B mixing-coordinate framework of Paper 4 V262 §S-Heff §3: the integer pair H ∈ {2, 3} cluster mixture supplies a continuous coordinate Heff, and the catalogue reports the corresponding bracketing interval. The mixing weights π are barycentric weights solved backwards from βobs on the pre-specified integer pair — an ex post diagnostic, not an independent derivation. Each Tier B row's detail panel surfaces this explicitly.

What this tool is not ▾

No user input. There is no CSV upload, no textarea, no drop zone, no parameter pulldown. All 22 rows are hard-coded from Paper 4 V261/V262 SI appendices. The tool does not classify networks, does not estimate H, and does not fit any exponent.

No graph alone → H inference. The same dense network can correspond to patents (H=2) or supercreative employment (H=3); recovering H from a bare adjacency list is structurally ill-posed. Paper 4's 22-row assignment is the author's domain reasoning over network type, substrate constraint, and branch direction — recorded here as a catalogue, not derived live.

Tool role. This is a disclosure visualisation of the Paper 4 ladder closure on the Bettencourt 22-panel. It is not a validator of arbitrary input networks.

About

Paper 4 — “A Relay-Depth Exponent Ladder Closing the Bettencourt 22-Indicator Urban-Scaling Panel”. A zero-fitted-exponent complementary closure with integer, continuous, and hybrid assignments.

Public on Zenodo (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20145297)

The ladder formula is β = 1 ± 1 / [H · ln(2H + 1)] with integer H; the branch sign (±) is determined by the indicator's network type and substrate constraint (physical infrastructure ⇒ −, socio-economic coordination ⇒ +, hybrid ⇒ context-dependent). For each row of the Bettencourt 22-panel the structural assignment of (network type, substrate, branch, H) is recorded by the author in V261 §S-PanelOrigins. This catalogue exposes that assignment row by row, with the relay chain made explicit as a sequence of 【】 boxes whose count equals H.
